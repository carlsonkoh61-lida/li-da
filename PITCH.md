# Li-Da — The Pitch

> The one honest problem Li-Da fixes, and the language that flows from it. Sell the problem, not the product. Living doc.

## The spine (canonical)

> Everyone online is telling you what to buy. None of them tell you why.
>
> Li-Da is the second opinion that shows you the reasoning — a clear, plain-English read on a stock, so you decide.
>
> No jargon. No FOMO. No one pushing you.

This is the source. Landing copy, the stranger test, the legal framing, and the product north-star all flow from these three lines. If a piece of copy or a feature can't trace back to them, it's drifting.

---

## The wound it names

The SEA beginner is drowning in noise — Telegram tips, FinTwit screaming BUY, YouTube red-arrow thumbnails — a flood of verdicts without reasons. There's no honest, calm place to think for themselves.

The pain isn't *"I lack data."* It's *"everyone gives me answers, nobody shows me the why, and I've no honest place to reason."*

Name the wound, not the toolset. The stranger doesn't feel "I need a fundamentals dashboard." They feel surrounded by people yelling buy and no one explaining anything.

---

## Why this framing (the decisions)

- **Problem-first, not product-first.** The hook is their pain (the noise), not "Li-Da is an app." A stranger reads their own life before they read a feature.
- **Chosen pain = the noise / verdicts-without-reasons angle.** Picked over the louder "you got burned" (regret) and the quieter "feeling dumb" (jargon-shame) because it's the most on-brand *and* the most differentiating. The other two are real wounds — they're kept as supporting notes in the body, not the headline.
- **Pain and cure share a word: why → reasoning.** The wound is "nobody tells you *why*"; the cure is "shows you the *reasoning*." Same idea, named twice — that's what makes it stick.
- **The differentiator is structural, not moral.** Li-Da wins by *showing the why*, not by claiming "everyone else lies." We don't attack the noise; we just do the thing the noise never does.
- **"Second opinion" = the promise, never the opening.** Leading with it would sell the product. It earns its place only after the wound is named.

---

## The spine does quadruple duty

One set of lines, four jobs — which is why it's the canonical source:

1. **Landing / hero copy** — the first thing a stranger reads.
2. **Validation hypothesis** — open the stranger test with *"none of them tell you why."* If they nod, the wound is real. (Ties to the ROADMAP M3 stranger validation.)
3. **Legal framing** — *"shows reasoning, you decide — never tells you what to buy"* is the anti-hype soul and the lightest-CMSA-compliance argument, unified in one sentence. (Ties to the M4 legal-consult gate.)
4. **Product north-star** — every feature gets tested against it: *does this help show the why, or does it sneak toward telling them what?*

---

## Supporting wounds (body copy, not headline)

Real pains, but they serve the spine — they don't lead.

- **Regret / FOMO — "burned by the hype."** The loud one. Use in body / secondary lines, where it deepens the noise wound rather than competing with it.
- **Beginner-shame — "you don't need to be an expert."** The SEA-wide one. Use as the reassurance / on-ramp — the warm hand that says you belong here, no jargon required.

---

## Standing rule

**Sell the problem, not the product.** Every enhancement revolves around the wound (the hyped, anxious SEA beginner with no honest place to think) and the SEA vision.

**Monetization north-star:** only charge when Li-Da genuinely helped. The model is decided *after* validation, not before — earn the trust, then earn the money.
